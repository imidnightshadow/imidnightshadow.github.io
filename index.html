The Ascent

We climbed until the air turned to lead, and the stars ceased their flickering. The city below was nothing more than a memory of light, a fading pulse in a vein of stone. Every step was a pact signed in breath, a silent agreement with the gravity that sought to claim our marrow.

The gargoyles at the summit did not watch us. They watched the horizon, where the void bled into the sea, waiting for the first sign of the Great Unmaking. We stood among them, stone ourselves, waiting for the word that would never come.

Source: Midnight Manuscripts Author: Anonymous
02

Echoes in Glass

"If you look too long into the mirror of the night, you might find that the reflection is the one who belongs here, and you are the ghost."

The mirrors in the manor of Crowne-Upon-Hill were never covered. They were taught to remember faces that had long since passed into the earth. When one walked the hallways at three in the morning, the echoes didn't sound like footsteps—they sounded like shattering glass, repeated ad infinitum.

THE ARCHITECT

The Architect of Shadows

Behind the veil of Shadows in Midnight is a singular obsession with the texture of darkness. For over a decade, I have curated the spaces where narrative and atmosphere collide, building worlds that exist only in the peripheral vision of the subconscious.

My work is a dialogue between the tactile and the ethereal. Each frame, each fragment of lore, is a brick in a gothic monument to the speculative unknown. I do not create for the day; I design for the hours that demand introspection.
